## The Hidden Costs to Watch For

Shopify is transparent about subscription costs, but there are additional expenses to factor in:

**Transaction Fees:** If you don't use Shopify Payments and opt for a third-party processor like Stripe or PayPal, Shopify charges an additional 0.6% to 2% per transaction depending on your plan.

**Credit Card Processing:** Even with Shopify Payments, you'll pay 2.5% to 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction depending on your plan tier.

**Apps:** Budget at least $50-$100/month for essential apps once you're up and running. More complex operations might spend $200-$500/month on apps.

**Themes:** While free themes exist, most serious stores invest in a premium theme ($150-$400 one-time cost).

**Domain Name:** $10-$20/year for a .com domain through Shopify.

For a real-world example: If you're on the Basic plan making $5,000 in monthly sales through Shopify Payments, here's what you'd actually pay:

- Subscription: $29/month (annual billing)

- **Total: ~$249/month** in platform costs, or about 5% of revenue

## Is Shopify Right for You?

Shopify makes the most sense if you:

- Want to launch quickly without wrestling with technical setup
- Plan to sell across multiple channels (online, social media, in-person)
- Need reliable hosting and security handled for you
- Value having everything in one integrated system
- Don't mind paying for convenience and professional support

Shopify might *not* be the best fit if you:

- Have extremely tight margins and can't absorb the transaction fees
- Need highly custom functionality that requires extensive development
- Want complete control over your hosting and infrastructure
- Are selling digital products only (platforms like Gumroad might be simpler)
- Have a tiny budget and need a completely free solution
